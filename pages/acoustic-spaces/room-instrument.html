The Room Instrument

The previous article turned a room into a small set of audio control signals: direct low/mid/high, routed low/mid/high, distance, material color, and media loss. This page uses those same signals as an instrument. There is less to read here because the point is to play it.

Drag the sound stones, listener, wall, and water. Horizontal motion chooses notes from a friendly scale. Vertical motion shifts rhythm and register. The room decides how much body, presence, and sparkle survive. A blocked direct ray is not just quieter; it becomes warmer, wetter, and more indirect.

The share button writes the whole room into the URL. Send that link and the recipient lands on the same composition: same stone positions, same listener, same wall and water, same scale, tempo, and material settings.

What is happeningThe article as a toy

Each stone asks the acoustic solver for a band-limited path to the listener. The green direct line is immediate audibility. The amber corner route is the slower routed energy. The wall mostly leaks lows. The water eats highs. Those values open filters, pan the notes, set delay/reverb weight, and choose how hard each note speaks.

The musical side is intentionally forgiving: pentatonic and modal scales, slow envelopes, sparse rhythms, and soft delay. That lets the acoustic movement do the expressive work. Move a stone behind the wall and it becomes a memory. Pull it through water and it loses its glitter. Bring it close to the listener and the room gives it back its edge.

Make it yoursSmall edits, large changes

Try one quiet bass stone near the listener, then put the chimes behind the wall. Move the water over only one path. Raise wall leak until the hidden notes are present but soft. Change scale last; the room usually matters more than the notes.
